How I Did It

1

Chose a Question

I noticed classmates spending a lot of time on phones and wondered if it was affecting grades. I framed a testable question: "Is there a correlation between daily screen time and GPA?"

2

Collected Data

Distributed a Google Form survey to 500 students across 3 schools. Students reported their daily screen time, GPA, grade level, and primary phone use. Data was collected over 2 weeks in October 2024.

3

Cleaned & Organized

Used Python and Pandas to remove incomplete responses (47 removed), fix formatting errors, and categorize students into screen time groups.

4

Analyzed & Visualized

Calculated Pearson correlation coefficient (r = −0.62), ran linear regression, and created scatter plots and bar charts using Matplotlib to visualize the relationship.

5

Drew Conclusions

Found moderate negative correlation between screen time and GPA. Wrote up findings, limitations, and recommendations for future research.

Reflection

The hardest part of this project was getting enough survey responses and ensuring students answered honestly about their screen time. If I were to redo this project, I would collect screen time data directly from phone settings rather than relying on self-reporting. I also learned that even a strong correlation doesn't mean one thing causes another — something I'll keep in mind for future data projects.
